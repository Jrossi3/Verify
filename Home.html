How I got hooked

When I was 10 years old, my brother challenged me to a game and informed me how to play. I quickly picked up how to play the game and use the pieces, but I struggled to get the hang of how to think in advance. As I played players that were better than me, I progressively understood how to think in certain situations. Starting young has been my biggest advantage over my opponents. Chess is a simple game of moving and taking pieces, but when learned, it has endless possibilities.

Background

I have been playing chess for 9 years. I have participated in local tournaments and have traveled to mutliple states to play. I have learned a great deal of chess and am self-taught. At Delbarton School, I was Chess Club president. I have a great deal of experience playing this strategy game and with it, I work to improve every day.

Why Chess?

Trains the Mind

Many people do not enjoy the art of the game because it is a very difficult game to understand. For anyone to indulge themselves in this, they allow themselves the capability to improve their mental age by up to 14 years and bolster brain function, memory, cognitive abilities, strategic thinking, and attention improvement. People who enjoy strategy games are bound to clutch onto Chess and never let go.

Complexity

Chess is very complex, but under the right environment, anyone can be given the key to unlock this beautiful game. The complexity is the reason it is often overlooked and never fully given a proper chance. I have found those who pursue strategic thinking games, cling to chess as they would their favorite video game. With the easy ability to play the game on our smartphones, there is ample opportunity to test the mind and grow.
